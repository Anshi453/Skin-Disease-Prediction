Common Skin Diseases

Acne

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It often causes whiteheads, blackheads, or pimples, and usually appears on the face, forehead, chest, upper back, and shoulders.

Eczema

Eczema is a condition that makes your skin red and itchy. It's common in children but can occur at any age. Eczema is long-lasting (chronic) and tends to flare periodically.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a skin disease that causes red, itchy scaly patches, most commonly on the knees, elbows, trunk, and scalp. It is a common, long-term (chronic) disease with no cure.

Rosacea

Rosacea is a common skin condition that causes redness and visible blood vessels in your face. It may also produce small, red, pus-filled bumps.
